two honeycomb lattices (A and B atoms with their three bonds, like graphene) rotated by ±θ/2 about an AA point — to scale: λ_moiré = a/(2·sin(θ/2)). With the relaxed lattice, the first-harmonic displacement field u±(r) = ±u₀·Σ_j b̂_j·sin(b_j·δ(r)) is applied (qualitative): the AA regions contract and the triangular AB/BA domains grow, with their domain walls

Conventions: ħv_F = 2.1354·a with a = 0.246 nm · k_θ = 2|K|·sin(θ/2), |K| = 4π/3a · q₀ = k_θ(0,−1) and ±120° rotations · plane-wave basis with circular cutoff of radius N_sh·|b₁| centered at q₀/2 (symmetric between layers) · mini-BZ: K = (0,0), K′ = (0,k_θ), M = (0,k_θ/2), Γ = (√3/2,1/2)k_θ. Relaxation: relaxed uses w_AA = 79.7 ≠ w_AB = 97.5 meV (corrugation reduces AA/BB tunneling and opens the flat-band gaps at Γ); rigid uses w_AA = w_AB = 97.5 meV (the central bands touch the remote ones). In the strip, the relaxed pattern applies the first harmonic u±(r) = ±u₀·Σ_j b̂_j·sin(b_j·δ(r)) with θ_loc(AA)/θ = 1+η, η = 0.605/θ²[°] (calibrated to ×1.5 at θ ≈ 1.1°; qualitative visualization). The Hermitian solver (Householder + QL) reproduces SciPy to within < 10⁻¹⁴ eV. With the relaxed parameters, the reference Python script places the minimum of W at θ* = 1.085° (validated: 1.05° ≤ θ* ≤ 1.10°).
